The Problem

Every civic tool was built for a different era.

Elections solve leader selection, not policy specification. NGOs suffer organizational capture. Polling has no execution layer. Social media optimizes for engagement, not deliberation.

The result is a coordination gap: diffuse citizen preferences cannot be converted into concentrated political force through any existing mechanism.

How Donations Work

The community decides how the money is used. Not us.

Donations go into a domain funding pool — not to specific proposals. The deliberation process determines how that pool gets allocated.

  • Participants deliberate on a policy issue and vote on proposals.
  • Winning proposals receive funding from the pool.
  • Every allocation decision is recorded in the public audit log and permanently verifiable.
  • The platform does not choose where the money goes — the deliberation outcome does.

This is why the deliberation process matters: the community decides how the money is used. That accountability is what separates this from a donation to an advocacy organization.
