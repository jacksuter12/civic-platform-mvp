The Problem

Every civic tool was built for a different era.

Elections solve leader selection, not policy specification. NGOs suffer organizational capture. Polling has no execution layer. Social media optimizes for engagement, not deliberation.

The result is a coordination gap: diffuse citizen preferences cannot be converted into concentrated political force through any existing mechanism.

How It Works

Deliberation that produces decisions, not discourse.

Phase 1

Open

Verified participants discuss the issue

Phase 2

Deliberating

Structured turn-based discussion with signals

Phase 3

Proposing

Formal proposals submitted and refined

Phase 4

Voting

Phase 5

Closed

Decision recorded to public audit log

🔒

Structured Signals

Support, concern, need info, block — instead of upvotes and downvotes. Signal the thread, not the post.

📋

Public Audit Log

Every phase transition, every vote, every allocation decision is publicly verifiable. No black boxes.

🛡️

No Algorithmic Feed

Posts shown chronologically. No engagement optimization. No outrage amplification.

⚖️

Execution Layer

Deliberation outputs connect to a hired lobbyist. The goal is legislative outcomes, not petitions.
