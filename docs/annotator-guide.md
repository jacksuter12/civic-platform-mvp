# Annotator Guide

You've been invited to review the Civic Power Consortium wiki — a draft document
that explains how the platform works. Your job is to read it and leave inline
comments on anything that's unclear, overstated, wrong, or missing. The author
reads every comment and uses them to improve the document before it goes public.
Your feedback doesn't need to be polished; honest reactions are more useful than
careful prose.

---

## How to access the wiki

Sign in at the platform URL using the email address you were invited with. You'll
receive a magic link — no password required. Click the link, and you're in.

Navigate to **/wiki** in the top menu. Pick any article to start reading.

---

## How to leave a comment

**On a specific phrase:** Highlight any text on the page. A small "Add comment"
button will float near your selection. Click it, type your comment, and submit.

**On a whole section:** Scroll to a section heading. Below the heading you'll
find an "Add comment to this section" button. Click it, type, and submit.

**To open the comment drawer:** Click the toggle button in the top-right corner
of any article. The drawer slides in from the right and shows all comments on
that page. You can also open it by clicking on any highlighted passage.

---

## Reactions and replies

Each comment has two reaction buttons:

- **Endorse** — "I agree with this / this is a good point."
- **Needs work** — "I disagree, or this needs more thought."

You can't react to your own comments.

To reply to a comment, click **Reply** on it. Replies are one level deep — you
can reply to a comment, but not to a reply.

---

## What happens to your comments

Every comment is visible to other annotators and, by default, to the public.
Every comment action is recorded in the platform's public audit log.

The document author reads all comments and uses them to revise the wiki. When
the document is edited, comments stay anchored to the text they were originally
attached to. If the exact text you commented on is removed or rewritten, your
comment becomes "orphaned" and appears in a separate section at the top of the
drawer so it doesn't get lost.

---

## What kind of feedback is most useful

**Specific over general.** "This sentence overstates the role of the
facilitator" is more useful than "I don't like this section."

**Substance over style.** Typos and copy edits are welcome but not the
highest-value thing. Focus on claims that seem wrong, confusing, or missing.

**Disagreements are welcome.** The point of the review is to stress-test the
document, not validate it. If you think a whole section is wrong-headed, say
so in a section-level comment with your reasoning.

---

## A note on cache

If the comment drawer behaves strangely after a recent update — highlights in
the wrong place, comments not appearing — hard-refresh the page
(**Ctrl+Shift+R** on Windows/Linux, **Cmd+Shift+R** on Mac). This forces the
browser to reload the latest version of the site code.
